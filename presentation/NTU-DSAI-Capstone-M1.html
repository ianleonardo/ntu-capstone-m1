6. Implementation

Business Understanding

Objective: Align curriculum with workforce demand by identifying high-competition sectors and skill gaps.

Target Users: Educators, career counselors, workforce planners.

Value: Reduce job-training mismatch through competition-focused market intelligence.

Data Acquisition

Source: Singapore job market data (Parquet format)

  • Job postings with vacancies, applications, views, salaries
  • Skills extracted from descriptions with temporal tracking
  • Sector categorization and experience requirements

Data Preparation

  • Cleaning: Remove nulls, standardize job titles (fuzzy matching), cap salaries (IQR)
  • Engineering: Calculate bulk factor (apps÷vacancies), segment experience levels
  • Optimization: Parquet format for fast I/O, cached aggregations

Exploratory Analysis

  • Competition Patterns: Apps/vacancies ratio reveals market saturation
  • Temporal Trends: Bulk factor changes identify emerging opportunities
  • Skill Demand: Top skills evolve monthly across sectors
  • Hidden Gems: High-vacancy, low-competition roles for job seekers

Implementation & Use Cases

For Educators:
  • Launch programs in low-competition sectors before saturation
  • Design curricula matching market experience requirements
  • Update skill modules based on trending demand
For Career Counselors:
  • Guide students to hidden opportunity roles (green quadrant)
  • Set realistic expectations for competitive markets
  • Recommend skill development based on temporal trends
For Workforce Planners:
  • Monitor bulk factor to predict talent supply/demand gaps
  • Track sector momentum for strategic resource allocation
  • Identify salary benchmarks by experience level
